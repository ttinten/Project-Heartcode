Effects of Flaming

Emotional Distress: Flaming often involves aggressive, hostile, or derogatory language, causing emotional distress such as feelings of anger, sadness, anxiety, or fear in the victim. Continuous exposure to such behavior can lead to significant emotional turmoil.

Post-traumatic Stress: Victims of severe and prolonged cyberbullying might develop symptoms of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). Flashbacks, nightmares, and heightened anxiety related to the traumatic experiences can persist long after the cyberbullying stops.


Suicidal Thoughts and Behaviors: In extreme cases, cyberbullying through flaming can push individuals towards suicidal thoughts or actions. The constant barrage of negative comments and harassment can make victims feel like there is no way out, leading to severe mental health crises.

Academic and Work-related Impact: Cyberbullying can affect one's concentration, productivity, and performance at school or work. The stress and emotional turmoil caused by flaming can lead to decreased academic grades or professional setbacks.


Trust Issues: Victims of flaming may develop trust issues, finding it difficult to trust others or form new relationships due to the betrayal and harassment they've experienced.

Stress-Related Symptoms: Prolonged exposure to cyberbullying can lead to physical symptoms such as headaches, stomachaches, sleep disturbances, and increased stress levels.
