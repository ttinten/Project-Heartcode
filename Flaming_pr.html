Impacts of Flaming

Impact #1 and #2

Emotional and Psychological Effects: Flaming can cause severe emotional distress, leading to feelings of humiliation, anger, anxiety, depression, and even suicidal thoughts. The constant barrage of hostile messages can significantly impact the mental health and well-being of the victim.

Low Self-Esteem and Self-Confidence: Being subjected to flaming can damage a person's self-esteem and confidence. Constant negative feedback and attacks can make victims doubt themselves and their abilities.


Impact #3 and #4

Social Isolation: Victims of cyberbullying may withdraw from social interactions both online and offline due to fear, embarrassment, or shame. This isolation can further exacerbate feelings of loneliness and depression.

Impact on Academic or Professional Life: Cyberbullying can interfere with a victim's ability to concentrate on their studies or work. It may lead to decreased academic performance, absenteeism, or difficulty in focusing on tasks.


Impact #5 and #6

Physical Health Implications: Prolonged exposure to cyberbullying can manifest physical symptoms such as headaches, stomachaches, sleep disturbances, and in severe cases, stress-related illnesses.

Cybersecurity Concerns: Flaming often involves sharing personal information, threats, or posting private content without consent, which can compromise the victim's privacy and safety. It can lead to identity theft, stalking, or further harassment.
